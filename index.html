Biography

Jason Matney is a PhD Candidate in the Center for Geospatial Analytics at NC State University. His research interests include standardization of novel geospatial methods for distribution to decision makers, the history of web-GIS as applied to natural resource conservation, and spatiotemporal Bayesian modeling. He teaches and develops online GIS courses, which he hopes will help introduce students to the world of geospatial analysis, while inspiring them to see the world in a new way.

Jason plans on defending his Dissertation during December, 2018, and is seeking both postdoctoral scholar and geospatial data science opportunities the world over.
