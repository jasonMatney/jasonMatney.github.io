Biography

Jason Matney is a PhD Candidate in the Center for Geospatial Analytics at NC State University. His research interests include standardization of novel geospatial methods for distribution to decision makers, the history of web-GIS as applied to natural resource conservation, and spatiotemporal Bayesian modeling. He teaches and develops online GIS courses, which he hopes will help introduce students to the world of geospatial analysis, while inspiring them to see the world in a new way.

Jason plans on defending his Dissertation during December, 2018, and is seeking both postdoctoral scholar and geospatial data science opportunities the world over.

Teaching

I am an instructor for the following courses at NC State University:

  • GIS 510: Fundamentals of Geospatial Information Science and Technology
    • This course provides an advanced overview of how geographic information systems [GIS] facilitate data analysis and communication to address common geographic problems. Students improve spatial reasoning and problem definition expertise while emphasizing geographic data models and structures, data manipulation and storage, customization through programming, and the integration of geospatial analysis and modeling into project-based problem solving applicable to a variety of disciplines. Skilled application of both desktop and cloud-based GIS software supports these areas. Extensive independent learning and computer experiences include virtual laboratory sessions, alongside optional online or in-person weekly help sessions to facilitate student learning.
  • GIS 595: Web GIS Frontiers
    • This course examines the design, development and deployment of web and mobile geospatial applications using internet and web-based protocols. Throughout the course, students will develop and deploy web and mobile GIS maps and applications relevant to their career using on-premises and hosted infrastructure. Course participants will be required to complete assignments with data relevant to their interests. Additionally, students will search for and examine scientific literature to understand how the course concepts are being employed and to foster ideas and discussion.
